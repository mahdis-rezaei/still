Update Still’s result quality. The current output is too literary, too long, and too focused on summarizing events. The journal quote should carry the emotional weight.

## Core correction

Still should not sound like an essayist.

Still should feel like a careful archivist who found the page worth returning to.

The journal itself is the poetry.

The AI’s job is to frame the page briefly, not rewrite it beautifully.

## New hard rule

The surfaced quotes should do at least 70% of the emotional work.

The AI observation should be brief, concrete, and evidence-based.

If removing the AI paragraph makes the result stronger, the paragraph is too long.

## Update all result writing

For every register — thread, memory, distance, value_signal, becoming, survival — change the output style:

### Observation length

Maximum 2–3 short sentences.

No long paragraphs.

No elaborate literary phrasing.

No “something moved across just a few days…”

No “as if she already knew they belonged together…”

No over-narration.

### Preferred structure

Label

Short date or time anchor

One simple framing line

Then quotes.

Then optional “Why this surfaced” in 1–2 short sentences.

## The engine should answer

Not:
“What happened?”

But:
“Why is this page worth returning to today?”

## For memory results

Do not summarize the sequence of events.

Instead identify the emotional hinge.

Example bad output:
“In the entries from November 2015, something moved across just a few days: a body in real pain on a meditation cushion, a fear named out loud to a teacher, and then a decision...”

Example good output:

Label:
A PAGE FROM THEN

Observation:
November 2015

This page is worth returning to because fear is not treated as something to escape. In the writing, fear becomes something to face, welcome, and listen to.

In your own words:

* “I asked him about my fear of being alone.”
* “Fear is part of me.”
* “Fear just wants my attention.”
* “Why not face it? Why not welcome it?”
* “He told me I am never alone.”

Why this surfaced:
This is not only a monastery memory. It is a page where a younger version of you began relating to fear differently.

## For thread results

Bad:
“Across the entries, the writer demonstrates a long arc of self-regulation and internal companionship...”

Good:
WHAT KEPT RETURNING

Across these pages, when fear or uncertainty appeared, you repeatedly tried to steady yourself from within.

In your own words:

* “Relax Mahdis relax. Take a deep breath.”
* “Everything is under control.”
* “Make sure YOU hold the pen.”
* “You got this Warrior.”
* “You can do it Mahdis.”

Why this surfaced:
The language changes, but the move stays the same: you talk yourself back toward steadiness.

## For distance results

Bad:
“In a sweeping arc from dependency to self-authorship...”

Good:
LOOK HOW FAR

This page matters because the earlier writing shows fear and dependency, while later writing shows more self-direction.

Then:

* Then: quote
* Later: quote
