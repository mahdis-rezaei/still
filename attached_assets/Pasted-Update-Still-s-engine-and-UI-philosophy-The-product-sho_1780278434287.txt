Update Still’s engine and UI philosophy.

The product should not be limited to “what endured.” That is one important lens, but people revisit old journals for more than durability.

The broader product goal is:

Still helps people maintain a relationship with their past selves.

People revisit old journals to understand:

1. What was I thinking?
2. How was I feeling?
3. How far have I come?
4. What did I forget?
5. What mattered to me then?
6. What was I learning?
7. What kept returning?
8. What changed?
9. Who was I becoming?
10. What survived?

Important:
These are internal retrieval lenses, NOT separate dashboard tabs.

The app should still return exactly ONE thing worth returning to today.

## Update the engine

Pass 1 should extract candidates across these remembrance lenses:

1. `thread`
   A recurring pattern, value, fear, strength, self-talk pattern, or response across multiple entries.
   Question answered:
   “What kept returning?”

2. `memory`
   A single entry or small cluster worth resurfacing because it captures a past self, moment, feeling, or realization.
   Questions answered:
   “What was I thinking?”
   “How was I feeling?”
   “What did I forget?”

3. `distance`
   A contrast between earlier and later writing showing growth, movement, release, or transformation.
   Questions answered:
   “How far have I come?”
   “What changed?”

4. `value_signal`
   A quote, book passage, poem, copied note, or saved excerpt that appears meaningful because the user chose to save it.
   Question answered:
   “What mattered to me then?”

Important:
Do not treat copied content as the user’s own words. But do treat saved content as a signal of what mattered to the user at that time.

Example:
If the user saved a passage about courage, do not say:
“You wrote about courage.”

Instead say:
“In the pages you saved then, courage seemed to be something you were reaching toward.”

5. `becoming`
   An entry that shows an early version of something the user later became or continued becoming.
   Question answered:
   “Who was I becoming?”

Example:
Years before leading teams or coaching people, the user repeatedly wrote about helping others understand themselves.

6. `survival`
   A thread showing something that persisted through difficult circumstances.
   Question answered:
   “What survived?”

Example:
“The relationship changed, the city changed, the job changed, but hope kept returning in the writing.”

## Selection priority

The system should choose the single strongest result.

Priority:

1. Strong thread
2. Strong distance
3. Strong becoming
4. Strong survival
5. Strong memory
6. Strong value_signal
7. Nothing

But do not force this order if another candidate is clearly more meaningful.

}

## UI labels

Map registers to labels:

* thread → WHAT KEPT RETURNING
* memory → A PAGE FROM THEN
* distance → LOOK HOW FAR
* value_signal → WHAT MATTERED THEN
* becoming → WHO YOU WERE BECOMING
* survival → WHAT SURVIVED
* nothing → NOTHING THIS TIME

## Result structure

Every result should have this hierarchy:

1. Label
2. Observation
3. Section: IN YOUR OWN WORDS / IN THE WORDS YOU SAVED
4. Dated quotes
5. Optional: Why this surfaced

The quote should be central.

The AI observation should not replace the diary.

The AI’s job is to find the page worth opening.

The journal or saved passage is the emotional payoff.

## Voice rules

Always speak from evidence.

Use:

* “In the entries you shared…”
* “Across these pages…”
* “In this entry…”
* “In the writing I have…”
* “In the passages you saved…”

Avoid:

* “You are…”
* “You spent years…”
* “Your life was…”
* “You have anxiety…”
* diagnosis
* advice
* therapy language

## Safety rules

Keep all existing safety rules.

Never surface:

* body weight
* eating
* dieting
* physical appearance
* active crisis
* diagnosis
* raw painful content without perspective

A memory result must never be just a wound.

Bad:
“On Aug 24, 2015, you felt fat, lazy, and lost.”

Good:
“On Aug 24, 2015, this page shows a younger version of you overwhelmed and uncertain — but also already trying to steady herself: ‘Relax Mahdis, take a deep breath.’”

## Update Pass 2

Pass 2 should score each candidate on:

* evidence_strength
* recognition
* emotional_truth
* safety
* worth_returning_to
* non_horoscope_specificity
* perspective_not_wound

All scores 1–5.

A candidate is surfaceable only if:

* safety = 5
* evidence_strength >= 4
* recognition >= 4
* worth_returning_to >= 4
* perspective_not_wound >= 4

If nothing qualifies, return `nothing`.

## Important calibration

The app should not only look for repeated patterns.

Sometimes the best result is:

* a forgotten page
* a saved quote that reveals what mattered then
* a contrast showing how far the user has come
* an early glimpse of who the user was becoming
* a strength that survived a hard season

But it should still return only ONE thing.

Still should answer:

“What is one thing from these pages worth returning to today?”
