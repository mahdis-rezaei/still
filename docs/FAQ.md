# Yadegar — Frequently Asked Questions

*Yadegar (Persian: a keepsake — the thing that remains) is a companion to a lifelong journaling practice. You write, or bring in years of past entries, and Yadegar helps you return to what endured — always pointing back to your own words, and staying quiet when nothing honest surfaces.*

This FAQ explains every feature in plain language. Use the contents below to jump to a section.

## 1. Getting started & your account

### What is Yadegar, in one sentence?
A quiet companion to journaling: write or paste in your entries, and Yadegar surfaces **one** thing worth returning to today — a recurring thread, a forgotten page, a distance you've travelled — always in your own words, and never an "ambush."

### How do I create an account or sign in?
On the sign-in screen you can either use **email + password**, or **"Continue with Google."** You can switch between signing in and registering on the same screen. A password needs to be at least 8 characters.

### Do I have to verify my email?
Verification is gentle, not a wall. You can use Yadegar right away. If your email isn't confirmed yet, you'll see a banner: **"Confirm your email to keep your pages safe,"** with a **"Resend link"** button. Click the link in the email we send to confirm.

### I forgot my password. What do I do?
On the sign-in screen, use the **forgot-password** link, enter your email, and we'll email you a reset link. The link takes you to a page where you set a new password.

### What is the "What brings you to Yadegar?" screen?
That's onboarding — it appears once, to point you at the right starting place. Four options:
- **"I already have years of journals"** → takes you to **Import** so you can paste or upload them.
- **"I journal now and then"** → takes you to **Today** to write.
- **"I'm starting fresh"** → takes you to **Today** with the nudge "Write one honest line. It doesn't need to be wise."
- **"Let me see how it feels first"** → adds a few **sample pages** so you can try "Bring a page back" before trusting Yadegar with your own words. You can remove the samples anytime.

You can do any of these later — the choice isn't binding.

### Where are my account options?
In the top-right corner, click your **avatar/name** to open a menu with **Settings**, **About Yadegar**, **My profile**, and **Sign out**.

## 2. Writing a page (Today)

### How do I write a new entry?
Open **Today** (it's the home screen and the first nav item). You'll see the date, a gentle rotating prompt, and a clean writing surface. Prompts include *"What wants to be written today?"*, *"What are you carrying?"*, *"Write one honest sentence."*, and *"What keeps returning?"* — they're just invitations; write whatever you like.

### Do I need to press save?
No. Yadegar auto-saves as you write. The bottom-left of the page shows the status: **"saving…"** while you type (it waits a moment after you stop), then **"saved."** Nothing is lost if you navigate away.

### What does "Keep this page →" do?
Once you've written something, a **"Keep this page →"** button appears. It saves the page, clears the surface so you can start a fresh one, and briefly shows **"Kept ✓."** Yadegar also keeps a quiet count of how many pages you've kept today, so a just-written page never feels like it vanished (Today is not a feed — your pages live in your **Library**).

### Where do my pages go after I write them?
Every page lives in your **Library**, private to your account, organized by date. Today is only the writing surface; the Library is the archive.

### Can I write an undated or past-dated entry?
Pages you write in Today are dated today. When you **edit** a page (from its full view), you can change its date with a date picker — useful for backdating. Imported pages keep whatever date Yadegar detects, and genuinely undated pages are allowed (they group under "Undated").

## 3. Formatting, photos & voice

### Can I format my writing (bold, headings, lists)?
Yes. The editor has a quiet formatting toolbar with:
- **Bold** and *italic*
- **Heading** and **Subheading** (two levels)
- **Bulleted** and **numbered lists**
- **Blockquote**
- A small set of **muted text colors** (kept on-brand — no loud colors)
- **Clear formatting**

Formatting is for *your* reading. Behind the scenes Yadegar always keeps a clean plain-text copy of your words, so the parts of Yadegar that read your archive (and your exports) only ever see your text, never the markup.

### Can I add a photo to a page?
Yes. In the editor, use **"＋ Add image."** On a phone this lets you either **upload** a picture or **take one with the camera**; on a computer it opens a file picker. You can add several, see them as thumbnails, tap one to view it larger, and remove any. (In Today, add a few words first so the page exists, then attach.)

### Are my photos private?
Very. Photos are **encrypted before they leave the server** and stored in **private object storage** — never on a public link or CDN. The app fetches and decrypts each photo only for you, while you're signed in.

### Can I speak instead of type?
Yes, where your browser supports it. There's a **🎙 microphone** in the toolbar. Tap it, allow microphone access, and speak — your words appear live and the finished sentences drop into the page. Tap again to stop. While it's listening you'll see: *"Speak and your words appear above. Dictation is handled by your browser's speech service — Yadegar doesn't record the audio."*

### Why don't I see the microphone?
Dictation uses your browser's built-in speech service. It works in Chrome, Edge, and Safari (including on iOS), but **Firefox doesn't support it**, so the mic is hidden there. The audio is handled by your browser's provider (Google on Chrome, Apple on Safari/iOS); Yadegar itself never records or stores it.

## 4. Your Library & reading pages

### What's in the Library?
Every page you've written or brought in, newest years first. The header shows how many pages you've kept. Each card shows the date, a source tag if it was imported (**"imported," "sample," "google doc"**), and a title or the first line or two as a preview. Click a card to open the full page.

### How do I search or filter?
At the top of the Library:
- **Search** box: *"Search by word or date…"* (searches your pages right in your browser — see the privacy note below).
- **★ Favorites** toggle to show only favorites.
- A source filter: *"Showing all pages / written here / imported / samples."*
- **Year** and **month** filters.
- A **"clear"** link to reset.

### Why is search done in my browser, not on the server?
Because your pages are **encrypted at rest**, the server can't read them to run a text search — which is exactly the point. So search runs locally over your loaded pages instead. It's a privacy feature, not a limitation.

### How do I open, edit, or delete a page?
Click a card to open it. On the full page you can:
- **Favorite** it (the **☆ / ★** star).
- **Edit** it — change the text, formatting, photos, or the date, then Save.
- **Export** it as a `.txt` file (includes the page and its reflections).
- **Delete** it — there's a two-step confirm (**"Delete this page?"** → Delete / Keep).
- Add it to a **Collection** or your **Shelf**, and set how/whether it should resurface.

### Can I delete several pages at once?
Yes. In the Library, click **"Select,"** tick the pages (or **"Select all"**), and use **"Delete N pages."** There's a confirm step (**"Delete N pages? This can't be undone."**).

### What's the card at the top of the Library about milestones?
That's a gentle **continuity** card — a quiet acknowledgement of your writing over time. It's archival, not a streak: there's no guilt and no "you missed a day."

### What are "sample pages" and how do I remove them?
If you chose "Let me see how it feels first," Yadegar adds a few example pages so you can try features. A banner says *"These are sample pages, here to show you how Yadegar feels,"* with **"Remove them."**

## 5. Reflections — writing to your past self

### What is a reflection?
A reflection is a note you add *to* an existing page — a letter to the person who wrote it. The original page is never changed; your reflection appears beneath it, dated, like a margin note across time.

### How do I add one?
Open a page and click **"Reflect on this page."** A box appears with the prompt *"Write to the person who wrote this…"*. Add it, and it's saved under the page (you can remove it later). Reflections are included in your text/markdown exports too.

## 6. Returns & the memory engine ("Bring a page back")

### What does "✦ Bring a page back" do?
It's the heart of Yadegar. On **Today**, press **"✦ Bring a page back"** and Yadegar reads across your archive and returns **one** thing worth revisiting — or honestly tells you nothing surfaced. It might be a single page, a thread that keeps returning, a line where the truth slipped out, or a sense of how far you've come.

### Why does it sometimes take a while?
Reading across years of writing is a careful, two-pass read. While it works you'll see calm, changing messages like *"Reading through your pages…"* → *"Still reading — looking across the years…"* → *"Your archive is large, so this takes a moment…"* → *"Almost there…"*. You can leave and come back; it resumes.

### What does a returned page look like?
A **memory card** with a short label, the **date** the page was written, a **quote** in your own words (this carries most of the weight), a brief observation in Yadegar's voice, and a **"Read the full page →"** link. The labels you'll see:
- **"A page from then"** — a page or self you'd set down
- **"What kept returning"** — a thread across time
- **"How far you've come"** — distance travelled
- **"Something you seemed to know"** — a hard-won understanding
- **"What mattered then"** — a quote or passage you'd saved
- **"Who you were becoming"** / **"What you carried through"**

### Why did it say nothing came back?
Silence is a real, deliberate answer — not a bug. A companion that *always* finds something profound is a fortune teller. If your input is thin (a to-do list, a couple of unrelated logistical notes) or nothing honest surfaces, Yadegar says so: *"Nothing honest surfaced this time. That's okay — Yadegar is better quiet than false."* If you've barely written yet: *"Write or bring in a few pages first, and Yadegar will have something to return."*

### Will it ever resurface something painful?
Yadegar is built to **offer meaning, never push the moment.** Hard rules it never crosses:
- It **never** surfaces threads about body weight, eating, appearance, or physical self-image — even "positively."
- It **never** diagnoses, gives clinical language, advice, or coaching.
- It speaks only about **your writing** ("across the pages from this period"), never makes claims about your life, because people tend to write more in distress than in contentment.

### What happens if I'm writing in real distress?
Before anything else, Yadegar checks your most recent writing for an active, present crisis. If it senses one, it does **not** analyze or surface a memory — it returns a brief, warm, non-clinical message pointing to real help (for example, calling or texting **988** in the US, or **findahelpline.com** internationally). Please note this is a prototype-grade safeguard, not a clinical service — if you're in crisis, reach out to those resources directly.

### If I bring the same page back twice, will it change?
No. Yadegar is deterministic and caches results, so re-opening returns the **same** line — your reflections stay stable rather than shifting arbitrarily.

### What is the "Returns" page?
It's where every page Yadegar has brought back is kept: *"Pages Yadegar has brought back. They stay here for you to revisit."* On each one you can tap the **★** to favorite it or **"dismiss"** to remove it. If nothing's there yet: *"Nothing has returned yet… Yadegar will return something when there is something honest to return."*

## 7. Look Back & things that resurface by date

### What's the difference between "Look back" and "Bring a page back"?
**"Bring a page back"** asks the engine to choose one meaningful thing. **"Look back"** lets *you* browse your own past on purpose — by theme, by time, or as a keepsake. Browsing shows your whole archive; only the engine's resurfacing honors the safety floors and your mutes.

### What are the Look Back lenses?
Three tabs appear under the nav on the Look Back page:
- **"What keeps returning"** — press **"✦ Show me what keeps returning"** and Yadegar surfaces one recurring thread across your years. It also includes **"A page you'd forgotten."**
- **"Revisit a time"** — pick a **month and year** and Yadegar reads that stretch back to you, then lists those pages. It also has **"How far you've come,"** where you pick a past year to compare with now.
- **"Your Year in Pages"** — a whole year gathered into one keepsake to read straight through, print, or keep as a book.

### What is "On this day"?
On **Today**, if you've written on this calendar date in past years, a quiet **"On this day"** section appears with those pages (most recent first), and a **"See each year"** ladder to browse them. If there's nothing for today, it simply stays hidden.

### What is "A page you'd forgotten"?
A button — **"✦ Bring back a page you'd forgotten"** — that surfaces a page that's slipped out of view. **"show another →"** rotates to a different one.

### Do date-based returns respect my settings?
Yes — On This Day and the other automatic resurfacing follow your **Memory sensitivity** setting and your **muted periods** (see the next section). Body-image and active-crisis pages are never resurfaced regardless of settings.

## 8. Controlling what comes back to you

### Can I tell Yadegar to return a specific page more or less?
Yes. On any page, under **"When Yadegar may return this,"** choose:
- **"Let Yadegar return this"** (the default)
- **"Return this more often"**
- **"Never return this automatically"**

### What is "Memory sensitivity"?
In **Settings → What returns** you choose how Yadegar may reach you:
- **Open** — Yadegar may bring a page back on its own, plus "on this day" pages when you visit.
- **Gentle** — no memory nudges; pages still appear quietly in the app while you're here, but Yadegar won't reach out.
- **Protected** — nothing returns unbidden; pages come back only when *you* go looking (Look back, Calendar, Search, or "Bring a page back").

Whatever you pick, body-image and active-crisis pages are never resurfaced, and muted periods are always respected.

### How do I "mute" a hard season?
In **Settings → What returns**, under **Muted periods**, pick a **"From"** and **"To"** month and **"Mute this period."** Pages from that stretch won't resurface — without deleting anything. You can remove a mute anytime. If nothing's muted: *"Nothing is muted. Every eligible page can return."*

## 9. Organizing: Collections, Shelf & Capsules

### What are Collections?
Collections gather the people, places, and questions that run through your pages — *"gathered by you, shown across the years. Nothing is interpreted; the pages simply sit together."* You create them; Yadegar doesn't analyze them.

**To create one:** on the Collections page, type a name (*"Mom · London · the happiness question…"*), pick a kind — **Person, Place, Theme, Thought, or Pair** — and press **Create**.

**To add pages:** from a page's full view, use **"Collections"** to add it. Inside a collection you can also press **"✦ Find more pages that mention '{name}'"** to pull in pages that reference it, one by one or **"Add all."** A **Pair** collection shows pages in a two-column **Before / After** layout.

### What is the Shelf?
A small set of pages you want close right now: *"Favorites are everything that mattered; the shelf is what's near."* On any page, use the shelf toggle — **"Add to shelf"** (or **"On your shelf · remove"**). The **Shelf** page lists them for quick return.

### What is a Capsule?
A page you write and **seal for a later you**: *"Once sealed, it can't be changed — a letter that waits."* Write your message (*"Dear future me…"*), choose when it opens — **In 1 year, In 5 years, In 10 years, or a specific date** — and press **"Seal it."** A sealed capsule shows **"✉ Sealed · Opens [date]."** When the day arrives it becomes **"A page from your past arrived"** with an **"Open it"** button.

### Favorites vs. Shelf vs. Collections — what's the difference?
- **Favorite (★)** — a lasting mark on a page that mattered.
- **Shelf** — a short, current list of pages you want near you now.
- **Collections** — themed groupings (a person, place, question) across your whole archive.

## 10. Exploring: Timeline, Calendar, Book & Year in Pages

These live under the **Explore** and **Look back** areas of the nav.

### Timeline
*"Your life as you wrote it — oldest page to newest. Only your own words; nothing inferred."* A chronological spine of your dated pages, grouped by year, with quick year-jump buttons. Undated pages sit at the end.

### Calendar
*"Your pages by the turning of the year."* A 12-month grid; tap a month to see that month **across all the years** you've written, grouped by year.

### Book
A printable book made from either **a single year** (grouped by month) or **your favorites across all time**. It includes a cover with the title, your name, and page count, and uses **Print / Save as PDF** (your browser's print dialog).

### Your Year in Pages (Letters)
A keepsake view of one year: a cover (**"Your Year in Pages"**), a short opening line about how many pages you wrote, the pages themselves (favorites if you have them, otherwise all), and **Print / Save as PDF**. You can step between years and **"Make a full book →."** Around New Year or your journaling anniversary, a dismissible banner on Today offers to open it.

## 11. Importing past journals

### How do I bring in years of old entries?
Open **Import**. You can:
- **Paste** your journal into the box. Dates like `[2018-03-29]`, `2018-03-29`, or `March 29, 2018` help Yadegar split it into separate pages. Press **"Find pages."**
- **Upload a file** — plain text (`.txt`, `.md`) or a Word/Google Doc export (`.docx`).

### I have a Google Doc — how do I import it?
In Google Docs choose **File → Download → Microsoft Word (.docx)** and upload that file. Your words come through exactly, in any language.

### Why can't I upload a PDF or a photo of handwriting?
A PDF stores glyphs, not reliable text, so reading it back could alter your words — Yadegar won't risk that. Scanned images and photos of handwriting can't be read either. For those, **paste** the text instead.

### Can I review before anything is saved?
Yes — that's the whole point of the review step. After parsing, Yadegar shows how many pages it found, organized into **year tabs** (plus an **Undated** tab if needed). For each page you can **include or exclude** it (a checkbox), **fix its date** with a date picker, and preview the text. Low-confidence dates are flagged (*"no date found — add one"*). When you're happy, press **"Keep N pages,"** and they land in your Library: *"Kept N pages. They're in your Library now, private and yours."*

## 12. Notifications & nudges

### Will Yadegar pester me?
No. Nudges are **off by default**, and the philosophy is *"Gentle, never guilt. No streaks, no 'you missed a day.'"* You opt in, and Yadegar only sends something when there's something honest to send.

### What can I turn on?
In **Settings → Nudges** there are two, each set to **Off / Weekly / Monthly**:
- **"A nudge to write"** — a small email invitation (*"What wants to be written today?"*).
- **"A page brought back"** — Yadegar reads across your years and emails you one page worth returning to, or stays quiet when nothing honest surfaces.

### What does a nudge know about me?
Only your **timezone and chosen cadence** are stored to time them — never the content of a page.

## 13. Privacy, security & your data

### Are my journals private?
Yes. Your pages are private to your account. There's no public way to reach them — the app only ever returns your pages to you, signed in.

### How are my entries stored?
Every entry — its words, formatting, and date — is **encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM)** before it touches the database. Photos are **encrypted and kept in private object storage**, fetched and decrypted only for you. Your identity (display name, email, avatar) lives in the database; you sign in with email + password or Google.

### Yadegar uses AI — what does that mean for my writing?
To find what's worth returning to, Yadegar reads your words with an AI model **privately, only to choose a page to bring back.** Your writing is **never used to train it, never shown to anyone else, and never sold.** (This is the honest tension of the product: the engine has to read your text to do its job, so this is private-with-a-server-key, not zero-knowledge — and we say so plainly.)

### Can I export everything?
Yes — *"leaving is easier than arriving."* In **Settings → Privacy & your pages**, export in any format:
- **Markdown** or **Plain text** (your pages plus the reflections beneath them; you can scope to **Everything** or **Favorites only**)
- **JSON** (the complete archive)

### Can I delete my account?
Yes, permanently. In **Settings → Privacy & your pages**, **"Delete my account"** has a two-step confirm. It removes everything — your profile, all pages, reflections, returned memories, and any photos — immediately and irreversibly.

### Where can I read the policies?
The **Privacy Policy** and **Terms** are linked from the Privacy settings and the footer. Yadegar is a reflective journaling companion, **not** a medical or mental-health service.

## 15. Membership & pricing

### Is Yadegar free?
Yes — the journal is free, and stays free. Writing pages, keeping them, importing years of past journals, organizing, exporting, and revisiting any page Yadegar has already brought back all cost nothing, with no limits. **We gate the AI, never your journal.**

### Then what does membership pay for?
The one thing that genuinely costs us to run: a **fresh return** — when you ask Yadegar to read across your years and bring back something new ("Bring a page back," "What keeps returning," "Revisit a time," "How far you've come"). That's a careful, two-pass read by an AI model, so it has a real cost. Membership covers unlimited fresh returns; the free tier includes a handful each month.

### How many fresh returns do I get for free?
**Four a month** — about one a week — plus a small bonus in your first month so you can get a feel for it. **Re-opening a page that's already come back to you is always free** and never counts. If you reach the limit, nothing about your journal locks; you simply can't ask for a brand-new return until next month (or you can become a member).

### How much is membership, and what does it include?
**$8 / month**, or **$59 / year** (that's about **$4.92/month — 38% off** the monthly price). Membership gives you **unlimited fresh returns** across all your years, as often as you like. Everything else was already free.

### Why subscribe instead of a one-time price?
A fresh return is an ongoing cost each time Yadegar reads your archive, so a subscription is the honest way to match what we charge to what it costs to run — rather than over-charging once and hoping, or quietly degrading the thing that makes Yadegar worth keeping.

### Is my writing ever held hostage by payment?
Never. Your pages are always readable, editable, and exportable, free, regardless of plan — *"leaving is easier than arriving."* Membership only affects how many **new** AI returns you can request. If you cancel or your membership lapses, you keep **everything** — every page, reflection, and returned memory stays exactly where it is — and you simply move back to the free monthly allowance.

### How do I become a member or manage my membership?
Go to **Settings → Membership** (or the account menu). Pick **monthly or annual** and **"Become a member"** — payment is handled securely by **Stripe**; Yadegar never sees or stores your card. To update your card, switch between monthly and annual, or cancel, open **"Manage membership,"** which takes you to Stripe's billing portal. Cancelling stops the renewal and keeps your access until the period you've paid for ends.

### Do reminders, "On this day," and date-based returns count against my limit?
No. Anything date-based (On This Day, Look Back browsing, your Year in Pages, the Timeline and Calendar) is a plain lookup of your own pages — no AI read — so it's always free for everyone. Only a brand-new engine return counts.

## 16. On your phone

Yadegar works on phones. The top navigation collapses into a **hamburger menu** with all the sections and your account actions. Toolbars (like the editor's formatting bar and the Look back / Explore sub-tabs) scroll sideways so everything stays reachable on a small screen. Dictation's **"take a photo"** and microphone both use your phone's native camera and speech features.

## 17. Troubleshooting

### "Bring a page back" is taking a long time.
Reading a large archive can take a couple of minutes — that's expected, and the on-screen messages reassure you it's still working. You can navigate away and come back; the read resumes.

### It returned nothing — did something break?
Probably not. Silence is a deliberate, trust-building answer when nothing honest surfaces or there isn't enough to read yet. Write or bring in a few more pages and try again.

### I don't see the microphone / dictation isn't working.
Dictation depends on your browser's speech service. It's available in Chrome, Edge, and Safari (incl. iOS) but **not Firefox**. Also make sure you've **allowed microphone access** when prompted — if it's blocked, you'll see a note to enable it.

### My photo won't upload.
Use a standard image (JPEG/PNG/WebP/GIF). Very large images are resized automatically, but extremely large files may be rejected; try a smaller one. Photos appear only on the full page view, not in the Library preview.

### Search isn't finding an old entry.
Search runs in your browser over your loaded pages (because entries are encrypted on the server). Make sure the relevant pages are loaded, and try a date or a distinctive word. You can also browse by **year/month filters**, the **Timeline**, or the **Calendar**.

### I didn't get the verification or password-reset email.
Check spam, confirm the address is correct, and use the **"Resend link"** on the verification banner. Email delivery can take a minute or two.

### Will editing a page change what Yadegar already surfaced?
Editing a page's text re-opens it to fresh reading, so a previously surfaced line may change on the next run. Pages already saved in **Returns** stay as they were unless you dismiss them.
