Replit Fix Prompt — Still: the observation must FRAME the line, not re-quote it
================================================================================

CONTEXT
In the product, a surfaced memory card renders the chosen QUOTE verbatim (large,
italic) and then renders the engine's OBSERVATION directly beneath it. So when
the observation repeats the same line, the user sees the quote twice — it reads
as redundant. Real example from production ("What keeps returning"):

  QUOTE shown on the card:
    "It sounds like your scope of your spirit collapse with your scope of your
     body! And they are not the same thing!"

  OBSERVATION shown right below it:
    "A coaching voice, mid-session — and suddenly this: 'your scope of your
     spirit collapse with your scope of your body. And they are not the same
     thing.' The whole page turns on that distinction."

The observation re-quotes almost the entire displayed line, then adds one short
clause. That repetition is the bug.

THE FIX (PASS-2 voicing prompt only — NO schema/contract change)
The observation must FRAME the displayed quote, not reproduce it. The card
already shows the words; the observation's job is to point at what they do.

Rules to add/tighten in the voicing instructions:
  1. Do NOT include the displayed quote, or any long verbatim run of it, inside
     the observation. Ban the "…and suddenly this: '<quote>'" / "There's a line
     where she says '<quote>'" pattern outright.
  2. Instead, point AT the line: name what shifts, what it holds together, or
     why it still lands — in your own words, without echoing the shown text.
  3. Keep every existing constraint: 1–3 sentences, concise (≤ ~45 words),
     varied opener (the "There's a line…" opener stays banned), no literary/
     analysis vocab, no interior/mind-reading claims, notice — don't interpret.
  4. Referencing a SECOND, non-displayed fragment (e.g. the far end of a
     cross-time thread) is still fine — the existing "only quote lines that are
     actually shown" rule still applies. The point of THIS fix is narrower:
     don't echo the one line already rendered above the observation.

BEFORE → AFTER (illustrative)
  Before: "A coaching voice, mid-session — and suddenly this: 'your scope of
           your spirit collapse with your scope of your body...' The whole page
           turns on that distinction."
  After:  "Mid-session, you draw a hard line between spirit and body — and the
           whole page pivots on insisting they aren't the same thing."

ACCEPTANCE / REGRESSION GUARD (already added to the harness)
scripts/src/eval/checks.ts now has a voice check:
  "observation doesn't restate the displayed quote"
It FAILS any observation that contains a contiguous verbatim run ≥70% of a
displayed quote (min 3 words) — so a fully restated short line like "Close your
eyes Mahdis." is caught as well as a long one. Run it live against the engine
after the prompt change:
  STILL_MODE=http STILL_API_URL=<engine base incl. /api> STILL_FRESH=1 \
    pnpm --filter @workspace/scripts run eval
The check should go GREEN once observations stop re-quoting the shown line. (The
recorded before-states catchup-2016-09 / breathe-2015-08 trip it on purpose —
they snapshot the old house style.)

This is a prompt-quality change. If it alters output text under a fixed
PROMPT_VERSION, bump PROMPT_VERSION so cached results don't serve the old voice.
